Autumn 2015-16: Report
5 Jan 2016
Another semester is now behind us. Here's an update of all the progress from the different teams

As we have moved to a new KTM 390 engine this semester, much of our work was adapting our existing systems to the new engine unit. The engine was mounted on the chassis on newly machined mounts welded to the original chassis. New air intake was 3D printed at Forgify, Delhi. A custom fuel tank and exhaust pipes were also manufactured and we have managed to get the engine running with the new parts. Muffler is a carbon fibre performance unit from Scorpion Exhausts.

A major hurdle was the cooling system underperforming in the new configuration. This is a known drawback of this particular engine model. We had decided to go ahead with the same radiator as seen in the KTM Duke 390 bike due to weight considerations. We intend to solve this problem by boosting mass flow through the same single radiator by adding a second fan in parallel with an aerodynamic sidepod and 3D printed shroud. Tests are going on but results have been positive till this point.

Once the engine worked in its stock settings, the next step was tuning. The engine has to be connected to the new RaceDynamics ECU via a new wiring harness. After some amount of debugging, the engine was running and fine tuning of parameters is going on as of now.

Differential is a lightweight LSD unit from Drexler. All other powertrain components incuding differential mounts, driveshaft and CV joints have been manufactured and installed on the car without a hitch.

This time we have shifted from a mechanical to a pneumatically actuated electromechanical gear shifting mechanism. This will greatly improve the drivability of the car compared to the previous years. Pneumatic unit is in assembly and testing stage currently.

Self-developed LCD Driver information panel and DAQ unit is also completed. We have implemented real-time wireless telemetry in this unit.

Vehicle Dynamics

The chassis of the car was completed well in advance. Mounting points for all components have been manufactured and are being welded to the chassis. Undertray, Impact attenuator and Firewall are also completed.

New Aluminium uprights have been machined. This time we have achieved a weight of 900g per upright. Lightweight wishbones are of mild steel and are also ready to go.

Wilwood brake system has been purchased and is awaiting assembly.
